Frequently Ask Question

How can I get service from Soul Support?

Soul Support is a telephone centered service. We do not provide face to service.

Who can call or contact us for support?

Students can reach out to us and let us know what kind of help they need. Whether they are struggling with academic stress, emotional challenges, or personal difficulties, once we receive their request, our team will review it and get back to them with a call to offer the necessary support and guidance.

Is this service free?

Yes, all the mental health support services provided by Serenity Space are completely free of charge for university students and staff. We aim to ensure that everyone has access to the help they need without any financial barriers.

What types of support do you provide?

We provide peer support, stress and anxiety management strategies, guided meditation, self-help resources, group support sessions, and referrals to professional counseling services.

Are the conversations confidential?

Yes, all conversations on our platform are strictly confidential. We ensure a safe and private environment for individuals seeking support. Your personal information and any discussions will not be shared with anyone outside the platform without your consent, except in cases where thereâ€™s an immediate risk to your safety.

What are the available support hours?

Our support hours are: Thursday to Saturday. Peer support is available during these hours.

Can I talk to a professional therapist through your platform?

Currently, we do not offer direct access to professional therapists through the platform. However, we can connect you with campus counseling services, where you can schedule a session with a licensed therapist or counselor.

Can I refer someone who needs help?

Yes, you can refer someone in need of help, but they must be our university students. You can share the website link with them or contact us to connect them with the appropriate resources. We encourage everyone to support their peers and look out for each other.

How do I know if I need mental health support?

If you're experiencing symptoms such as persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, excessive stress, difficulty focusing, changes in sleep or appetite, or if you're feeling disconnected from others, it might be a sign that you could benefit from support. It's okay to seek help if you're feeling overwhelmed or need guidance. Our platform is here for you.

How can I support this initiative?

You can support this initiative by spreading awareness about mental health, sharing our resources with fellow students, and encouraging those in need to seek help. Additionally, your feedback can help us improve our services and make them more effective for the university community.
